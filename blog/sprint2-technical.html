What is the difference between Margin, Border and Padding?

An exploration of the box model

10 September 2020


What is the css Box Model?

In order to understand the difference between margin, border and padding you should first understand the box model and think of an element on a website as consisting of 4 nested boxes. Margin is the outer box, then border, then padding and finally the content box which holds the meat and potatoes of the element.

Content

This is the origin from which you are sizing and spacing this element on the page. Margin, border and padding are styled in relation to the content box. The differences we will explore between margin, border and padding will all be in relation to how those elements interact with the content they are surrounding.

Padding

This is the amount of space inside the box between the content and the border. Increasing the padding pushes the border away from the content.

Margin

Border

Padding

Content


Border

This is the boundary of you content box. It can visually represent the container that is holding your content within the element and is the component of the element that separates the padding from the margin.

Margin

Border

Padding

Content


Margin

This is the amount of space between the border and other elements on the page. If you have two separate elements containing content, the margin determines how much space is between the borders of the two elements. Unlike the padding, which determines the space between the content and its own border, the margin determines space outside of the border.
